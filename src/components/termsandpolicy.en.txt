The Helia Postpartum Care Center Terms and Policy

These terms may be updated depending on operational circumstances.
Services are provided under the terms in effect at the time of contract.

Section 1. Purpose
These terms define the contractual conditions between The Helia Postpartum Care Center ("Operator") and users ("Mother and Guardian") regarding the use of postpartum care services.

Section 2. Amendment of Contract
The Operator may amend this contract as needed.
However, existing reservations continue under the contract in effect at the reservation date.

Section 3. Formation of Contract
A reservation is considered valid when the contract is provided, the user agrees, and the deposit is paid.
1) The deposit is 10 percent of the total fee.
2) The remaining balance must be fully paid on the day of check-in.
3) The stated fee is based on one mother and one newborn. Additional charges may apply if conditions change.

Section 4. Use of the Center
1) The standard room assignment is a private room for one mother or a mother-baby room.
2) Check-in may be delayed if full occupancy occurs due to delivery schedule changes.
2.1) The estimated delivery date refers to the date recorded in the maternity record booklet.
2.2) If birth occurs within seven days before or after the expected date but check-in is unavailable, the center covers hospital admission costs during waiting, up to KRW 200,000 per day for up to three days.
2.3) If birth occurs outside the seven-day range, waiting-period hospital costs are not covered. Check-in is prioritized when a vacancy occurs.
3) Unused spa or prenatal care due to user circumstances cannot be converted into postpartum services.
4) Final room assignment on check-in day is discussed with the user, but operational assignment by the center takes priority.

Section 5. Service Period and Contract Adjustment
1) Standard stay is 13 nights and 14 days, and may be adjusted by mutual agreement.
2) Standard check-in starts at 11:00 AM, and check-out is by 10:00 AM, unless adjusted by agreement.
3) Extended check-out fees: within 6 hours, 50 percent of one-day fee; after 6 hours, 100 percent of one-day fee.
4) Basic prenatal and postpartum services by room type expire at check-out if unused.

Section 6. Eligibility Restrictions
Admission is restricted in the following cases:
1) Mother, newborn, or guardian with a contagious disease.
2) Newborn under 2.5 kg at birth.
3) Newborn with gestational age under 37 weeks.
4) Any case deemed unsuitable by the center, including substance-related or severe mental health concerns.

Section 7. Deposit Refund Before Admission
1) Users may cancel before check-in. Deposit refund is based on cancellation timing:
1.1) 91 days or more before due date, or within 24 hours after contract: 100 percent refund.
1.2) 61 to 90 days before due date: 60 percent refund.
1.3) 31 to 60 days before due date: 30 percent refund.
1.4) 30 days or less before due date: no refund.
2) If prenatal services included in the package were used, the regular price per use is deducted from the deposit before refund.
3) For cases under Section 6 that were unforeseeable at reservation time, the reservation is treated as invalid and the full deposit is refunded.

Section 8. Contract Termination After Check-In
1) Users may terminate after check-in if attributable reasons of the Operator occur:
1.1) Intentional misconduct or negligence causing injury to mother or newborn.
1.2) Infectious disease caused by negligent management.
2) In such cases, the center refunds the remaining amount after deducting actual usage at regular rates, and additionally compensates 10 percent of total fee.
3) No refund for voluntary early checkout without Operator fault.
4) If user obligations under Section 13 are violated, the Operator may request checkout without refund.

Section 9. Refund Rules
1) Refund before check-in follows Section 7.
2) Refund after check-in follows Section 8.
3) For separately paid spa or esthetic services, refunds during use are calculated after deducting regular price per used session.

Section 10. Fees
1) Fees are based on one mother and one newborn.
2) Full remaining balance is due at check-in.
3) If only the mother checks in, newborn fee is not deducted. If newborn check-in is delayed due to hospital admission, KRW 30,000 per night is deducted for delayed days.
4) For multiple births, KRW 1,800,000 per additional newborn is charged for a two-week stay.
5) Standard two-week room rates: VIP KRW 5,500,000 / VVIP KRW 6,500,000 / Prestige KRW 9,500,000.
6) Stay extension fee per night is total fee divided by 13, rounded down to the nearest KRW 1,000.
7) Guardian meal fee is KRW 20,000 per meal with advance request deadlines.

Section 11. Maternal Care Services
Basic services may be adjusted for quality improvement and include:
1) Maternal and newborn care.
2) Facilities and equipment related to maternal recovery.
3) Meals and snacks for the mother.
4) Designated formula, diapers, and bottles for newborn care.
5) In-house programs.
6) Spa and room-specific services.
7) Basic breastfeeding education and support environment.

Section 12. Operator Obligations
1) The Operator maintains staffing and facilities required by applicable maternal and child health law.
2) In case of infectious disease, affected users may be required to check out to prevent spread.
3) The Operator monitors health conditions and informs users, but is not a medical institution and cannot provide medical treatment.
4) If infection or disease is suspected, transfer to a medical institution may be arranged based on user decision.
5) Personal information is not disclosed to third parties without consent.

Section 13. User Obligations
1) Users must comply with facility and safety rules and avoid behavior that harms other users.
2) Users must disclose relevant health conditions of mother and newborn.
3) Users must comply with infection-control principles, including hand hygiene and guardian-entry rules.
4) If the mother has infectious symptoms, check-out may be required to protect newborns.
5) Medical examination decisions are made by the mother or guardian.
6) Injury caused by user negligence is the user's responsibility.
7) One guardian must be designated before check-in.
8) Guardian changes are generally not allowed unless mutually agreed.
9) Users must not damage facilities and must compensate for proven damages.
10) External food and outside outings are restricted for hygiene and health reasons.
11) Personal electrical or medical support devices are restricted for safety.
12) Users must follow all notices and usage guidelines.

Section 14. Epidemiological Investigation and Temporary Suspension
1) If an infectious case is identified, local health authorities may conduct an epidemiological investigation.
2) If operations are temporarily suspended by legal or administrative order, forced checkout refunds are settled according to applicable rules.

Section 15. Voluntary or Forced Checkout
1) No refund for voluntary early checkout without Operator fault.
2) If contagious disease occurs, forced checkout may be required to prevent spread.
3) Users who disrupt operations or pose risk may be required to leave.
4) If the mother is discharged due to health issues requiring hospitalization, both mother and newborn may need to leave together.

Section 16. Damages
1) If loss occurs due to infectious disease during stay, compensation may be provided upon submission of objective medical documents, unless Operator fault is disproven.
2) The Operator is liable for proven damages caused by facility or equipment defects.
3) If a guardian brings infection from outside and causes damage in the center, the user may be liable.
4) The Operator maintains liability insurance as required by law.
5) If users disseminate false or exaggerated harmful claims without clear proof of intentional misconduct or gross negligence, the Operator may seek legal compensation.

Section 17. Exemption
1) The Operator is not liable for damages caused by force majeure events.
2) The Operator is not liable when damages occur because users fail to comply with these terms.

Section 18. Belongings and Responsibility
The Operator is not responsible for loss or damage of valuables unless intentional misconduct or negligence is proven.

Section 19. Jurisdiction
Any legal dispute related to this contract is subject to the jurisdiction defined by applicable civil procedure law.
